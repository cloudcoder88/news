California DMV apologizes for license plate that some say mocks October 7 attack on Israel

The California DMV apologized for a personalized license plate that was perceived as displaying hate speech related to an attack on Israel, but the owner's relative claimed it was a misunderstanding.

The owner's son confirmed that the license plate, "LOLOCT7," was intended to depict the owner as a Filipino grandfather with seven children, despite a monitoring group reporting it made reference to the October 7 attack on Israel.

The DMV is recalling the offensive license plate and will strengthen its internal review process to prevent such oversights in the future.

On X, the DMV issued a statement Thursday, saying the department is "taking swift action to recall these shocking plates, and we will immediately strengthen our internal review process to ensure such an egregious oversight never happens again
